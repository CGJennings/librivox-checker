Volume

Checker tests how loud your recording will sound to the listener, and it will tell you if this falls outside of LibriVox guidelines. A recording's loudness roughly corresponds to the average height of its waveform. (Checker uses a more complex method that better models human hearing.)

Increasing Volume

If Checker tells you that your recording is too quiet or loud, you can fix the problem using the free MP3Gain application. The default settings of the this application will create files that match the target volume for LibriVox files.

When you amplify your recording, you also amplify the background noise. If the recording is very quiet, then you won't be able to clean the noise without distorting the sound of your voice. In this case, you may need to re-record after adjusting your recording level.
